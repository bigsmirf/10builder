Canonical definition

This is the official language that defines MGS across the product, documentation, and future public references.

MGS™ (Moore Global Services) is a managed infrastructure and deployment platform that powers digital applications, websites, and services through a fully controlled, scalable, and secure environment. MGS abstracts complex infrastructure layers, including hosting, networking, and deployment, into a unified system that allows users and organizations to build, launch, and operate without direct server management.

MGS is not the product users come to touch first. It is the infrastructure reality that makes controlled ownership possible behind the scenes.

What MGS powers

MGS is the engine layer beneath the experience layer. Users interact with products like 10Builder, but the hosting, deployment, and systems control live underneath through MGS.

Publishing

Managed site deployment

MGS powers the publish flow that turns a project into a live hosted site without exposing command-line complexity, server configuration, or infrastructure overhead to the user.

Hosting

Controlled delivery layer

MGS provides the environment in which websites, applications, and digital services are hosted, organized, and served through a controlled operating model.

Operations

Infrastructure abstraction

MGS hides the operational layers users do not need to touch while preserving the ability for the system owner to maintain security, compliance, performance, and scale.

Why MGS exists

MGS exists because most people do not actually want raw hosting. They want ownership, control, and results without having to become infrastructure operators.

  • To restore control without forcing technical overhead. People should be able to own what they build without memorizing server commands, deployment steps, or hosting workflows.
  • To standardize a scalable operating model. MGS makes it possible to deploy and manage digital projects through one controlled system rather than fragmented ad hoc infrastructure.
  • To create a future-proof abstraction layer. Today MGS may be backed by third-party infrastructure providers. Over time, MGS can evolve into Moore-controlled infrastructure without forcing a visible platform leap for users.

Role in the stack

MGS is the infrastructure layer in a larger ecosystem. It is the hidden engine, not the front-facing product experience.

10Builder
The user-facing creation and publishing experience.
MooreSoft
The systems and strategic logic layer shaping how products, controls, and workflows operate together.
MGS
The managed infrastructure and deployment layer powering hosting, publishing, and controlled delivery behind the scenes.
Current State

MGS as managed infrastructure abstraction

MGS may currently utilize external providers such as AWS and Cloudflare as underlying suppliers while preserving a branded, controlled infrastructure identity at the product layer.

Future State

MGS as Moore-controlled infrastructure

As scale grows, MGS can transition from abstraction over third-party infrastructure into a more directly controlled global services layer without changing the user-facing experience.

MGS exists so products can feel simple while infrastructure stays disciplined.

The goal is not to expose more complexity. The goal is to create an environment where digital ownership, managed deployment, and scalable control can operate as one system.
